# Lab 04 – User Persona

## 1. Persona Name

**Andrew, Year 2 Computer Science Student**

---

## 2. User Type

Student who needs to borrow academic equipment and find student services for coursework and projects.

---

## 3. Background and Context

Andrew frequently works on programming assignments, multimedia projects, and presentations. He often needs academic equipment such as DSLR cameras, Arduino kits, calculators, or classmates with programming, design, and tutoring skills before assignment deadlines.

He experiences this problem when he has urgent coursework or project tasks but cannot quickly find available equipment or reliable students who can provide services.

Andrew already uses digital tools such as university websites, mobile applications, messaging apps, online search, LINE, Facebook, Discord, and digital marketplaces.

---

## 4. Goals

Andrew wants to:

- Find academic equipment quickly.
- Request help or services from skilled classmates.
- Save money compared to renting equipment off-campus.
- Complete coursework and projects more efficiently.
- Use a trusted platform limited to RSU students.

---

## 5. Pain Points from Lab 03 Evidence

| Pain Point | Evidence ID / Respondent | Explanation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Difficulty finding equipment | E01, E02, E03 | Students often need calculators, cameras, Arduino kits, or learning resources but do not know who has them available. |
| Information is scattered across informal channels | E04, E05 | Students currently search through LINE groups, Facebook groups, Discord, and personal contacts, which makes information difficult to find. |
| Difficulty finding student services | E06, E07 | Students struggle to find classmates who can provide tutoring, programming help, design support, or video editing services. |
| Lack of trust | E08, E09 | Students prefer a verified student-only platform because borrowing equipment or requesting services from unknown people can feel risky. |
| Time wasted before deadlines | E10 | Students may spend too much time searching for equipment or help instead of completing coursework. |

---

## 6. Current Alternatives / Workarounds

| Current Alternative | Weakness / Limitation | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ask friends or classmates | Limited to personal network and not always reliable | E01, E02 |
| Post in LINE or Facebook groups | Posts can be missed or buried quickly | E03, E04 |
| Contact seniors directly | Depends on knowing the right senior student | E05 |
| Borrow from classmates | Availability is uncertain | E06 |
| Rent equipment outside the university | Can be expensive and inconvenient | E07 |

---

## 7. Design Implications

The RSU Nexus platform should support:

- Verified student accounts using RSU email.
- Searchable equipment and student service listings.
- Categories for academic equipment, learning resources, and student services.
- Listing details with availability status.
- Request or contact function for students.
- Basic notifications or confirmation messages.
- A trusted campus marketplace experience for RSU students.

The most important user need for the MVP is:

> Students need a fast and trusted way to find academic equipment and student services within the university.
