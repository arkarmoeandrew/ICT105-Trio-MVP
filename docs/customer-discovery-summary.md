# Customer Discovery Summary

## Project Title

RSU Nexus – Campus Resource Marketplace

## Target Users Studied

- RSU Students
- First-year students
- Second-year students
- Third-year students
- Final-year students
- International students
- Student club members

## Number of Responses / Interviews

15 Student Responses

## Main Repeated Problem

Students often need temporary access to academic resources, equipment, or specialized skills, but there is no centralized and trusted platform to help them find these resources efficiently.

Many students currently depend on friends, social media groups, or personal networks, which makes the process slow, inconsistent, and difficult to manage.

## Main Current Workarounds

Students currently solve the problem by:

- Asking friends and classmates
- Posting in Facebook groups
- Using LINE group chats
- Contacting senior students
- Asking club members
- Borrowing directly from personal networks

## Strongest Evidence Found

- Most respondents reported needing resources such as calculators, textbooks, cameras, chargers, and project equipment.
- Students spend between 15 and 50 minutes searching for resources or assistance.
- Most respondents preferred a university-exclusive platform with student verification.
- Many students expressed willingness to share resources and skills if a trusted system existed.
- Students also need access to skills such as programming, tutoring, graphic design, and video editing support.

## Weak Evidence or Unclear Findings

- Not all students experience resource shortages frequently.
- Some students prefer asking close friends rather than using a platform.
- A few respondents indicated they would only use the system when facing urgent needs.
- Long-term adoption rates remain uncertain without real-world testing.

## What We Must Revise

- Add student verification using RSU email accounts.
- Include both resource-sharing and skill-sharing features.
- Improve trust through user profiles and activity history.
- Focus on high-demand resources and services during the MVP stage.
- Design a simple and searchable platform interface.

## Decision for Next Lab

Continue with same problem direction.

The collected evidence confirms that students face difficulties finding resources and peer support efficiently. The team will proceed with the RSUNexus concept and begin developing the MVP prototype.

## Updated Problem Statement

RSU students frequently need temporary access to academic resources, equipment, and specialized skills, but currently rely on fragmented channels such as friends, social media groups, and personal networks. This creates delays, increases search effort, and limits access to valuable resources. A trusted university-exclusive platform is needed to connect students, resources, and skills in a more efficient and organized way.
